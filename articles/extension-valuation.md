# How Chrome Extensions Are Valued as Real Businesses in the Acquisition Market

Chrome extensions are real businesses that can be valued, bought, and sold. Most indie developers never think about what their extension is worth, but there is an active acquisition market and understanding valuation helps you make better decisions even if you never sell.

## Revenue-Based Valuation

The standard range for extension valuations is 2x to 4x annual revenue depending on growth and stability. Subscription revenue is valued higher than one-time purchases because it is predictable and signals recurring demand. Lifetime deals actually reduce valuation because they front-load revenue and create future support obligations without future income. A $10K/year extension with subscriptions might sell for $30-40K while the same revenue from one-time sales might only fetch $20-25K.

The revenue multiple you can command depends heavily on the predictability of that revenue. Buyers pay a premium for MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue) because it indicates stable, ongoing value delivery. When evaluating your own extension, consider what your revenue streams look like from a buyer's perspective.

## User-Based Valuation for Pre-Revenue Extensions

Not all valuable extensions are generating revenue yet. For pre-revenue extensions, the rough valuation range is $0.50 to $3.00 per weekly active user depending on the category and engagement. A productivity extension with 10,000 WAU and strong engagement could be worth $15-30K even with zero revenue because the buyer sees clear monetization potential.

Buyers value engaged user bases because they represent optionality. A large, active user base gives the new owner multiple paths to monetization, whether through freemium upgrades, premium features, or advertising. The key metric is not just raw users but engaged users who actively use the extension week after week.

## What Increases Valuation

Several factors can significantly boost what buyers will pay for your extension:

- **Consistent growth**: Month-over-month growth in users and revenue signals product-market fit and future potential
- **Strong ratings**: A rating of 4.5 stars or above signals product quality and reduces buyer risk
- **Low churn**: For subscriptions, retaining users over time proves lasting value
- **Diversified traffic**: Not dependent on a single channel, reducing concentration risk
- **Clean code**: Well-documented code that a buyer can maintain without a complete rewrite
- **Transferable brand**: A recognizable name and domain that carries value
- **Email list**: An engaged mailing list represents direct access to users for launches and updates

## What Decreases Valuation

Conversely, these factors will reduce what buyers are willing to pay:

- **Single traffic source**: Dependence on one channel creates vulnerability
- **Declining users**: Even if revenue is stable, shrinking user numbers suggest future problems
- **Technical debt**: Messy code that would require a complete rewrite adds cost and risk
- **Manifest V2**: Still running on the old manifest version means migration work ahead
- **Policy violations**: Any history of Chrome Web Store policy violations raises red flags
- **High refund rates**: Indicates product issues or mismatched customer expectations

## Valuation Multiples by Category

Different extension categories command different valuation multiples:

- **Developer tools**: 3x to 5x revenue. These extensions have sticky users and high willingness to pay. Developers treat their tools as essential infrastructure and will pay premium prices for quality.
- **Productivity extensions**: 2x to 4x revenue. Solid fundamentals with reliable demand. These extensions solve real problems and often have clear monetization paths.
- **Social and entertainment**: 1x to 3x revenue. Users are more fickle and monetization is harder. These extensions often struggle with engagement consistency and ad revenue volatility.

## Getting a Professional Valuation

If you want to explore selling your extension, several platforms specialize in Chrome extension acquisitions:

- **Acquire.com**: Provides structured listings with verified financials and a professional process
- **MicroAcquire**: Connects you with vetted buyers looking for smaller properties
- **Digital asset brokers**: Handle the full process for a commission, useful for larger extensions

For a quick rough estimate, take your trailing 12-month revenue and multiply by 3 as a starting point, then adjust based on the factors above. This gives you a baseline to work from when evaluating strategic decisions.

## Putting Valuation into Practice

At zovo.one, we use valuation thinking to prioritize development investment across our portfolio of 17 extensions. Understanding what drives value helps us decide which extensions deserve more resources. We treat the portfolio as a diversified asset rather than a collection of side projects.

When you understand what makes an extension valuable, you can build with those factors in mind from the start. Focus on engagement, maintain clean code, diversify your traffic sources, and build genuine value that would matter to a potential acquirer—even if you never plan to sell.
